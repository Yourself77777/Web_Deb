---Counter Claim---

Whether a hot dog qualifies as a sandwich is a subject of passionate, ongoing debate, largely revolving around the precise definition of the latter term. The core argument for classifying a hot dog as a sandwich rests on a broad, literal interpretation: a sandwich generally consists of meat (or a filling) placed between two pieces of bread or inside a sliced bun [1]. Since a frankfurter is a processed meat product served in a longitudinally sliced bun, it perfectly fits this structural description [1]. Even institutional authorities lend credence to this view; both the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) and the dictionary publisher Merriam-Webster have definitions that logically include the hot dog under the sandwich umbrella
